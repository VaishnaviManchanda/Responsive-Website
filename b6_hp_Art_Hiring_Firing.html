The Art of Hiring and Firing

What do you mean by a successful team? An exceptional team does more as a group than it does individually. If you have the bunch of talented people with you, you are always at the mercy of the life events. Now you want to be good manager and lead a group of team players around a common purpose. But to do this, you need to hire people who have common traits.

There are 3 traits of team player:

1. Team players are ‘Humble’: By humble, It doesn’t mean that, they are not confident or not self-assured. It means that they think selflessly. They are open to new ideas. They are willing to sacrifice what they need in that moment because it benefits someone else or your team.
2. Team players are ‘Hungry’: They have goals and they go after them. As a manager, you want hungry people who are pushing themselves and their peer forward. That’s how you achieve great things.
3. Team players are ‘People Smart’: They have emotional intelligence to manage their own emotions and to build healthy, relax interactions with other people.
It’s important that you get all three of these traits. They balance each other out.

Let’s consider the following cases now,

If you hire a person who is only ‘Hungry’, then he is going to be a “The Bulldozer”. They are going to do what they want to do to succeed. And as they are only having hunger, the most important thing is they will lack in execution. Everytime, they will feel like doing anything to achieve something but as they not humble and people smart they are gonna drag other people and so your team.

If you hire a person who is ‘Humble’ and ‘Hungry’ but lack in people smart are like “The Accidental Messmaker” but as they are not people smart, they might be rude. They might be abrasive, passive aggressive, they might miss the social cues or they might violate social norms. This persons cannot form healthy interactions, interpersonal relationships and without those its hard for a team to mix up.

Now, if you hire a person who is ‘Humble’ and ‘People Smart’ are “The Loveable Slacker” This people can maintain healthy relationships as well as they get selfless and open to new ideas but as they lack in hunger, they become the most real damaging people in the company. Because lack in hunger results in incompetency which is not good for any team and as they are not competent to others as well as they establish strong relationships, it gets very difficult to fire such people from team. They help the team, they are willing team mates, they are people smart but they don’t posses any sign of growth, they are not gonna deliver much on their own as well as they are not gonna push their team to achieve more either.

One of the most danger combination of trait is, ‘People Smart’ and ‘Hungry’, they can be termed as, “The Skillful Politician”. This people are politicians. They have a strong desire to achieve their goals and they are good at working with people. They are gonna manipulating people. But, everything they do is for their own hunger and goal. They are not sacrifiece their anything for team. They are not interested in helping on picking on new ideas that benefit someone else.

So, how do you test for those traits- Traditional methods of interview where you are sitting on a desk asking some same questions like what are your strength and weaknesses? Where you want to see yourself in next 3 years is not going to help. I mean, its great if you want to hear them say “I am a team player” in those exact words. People really practise their interview settings. They are going to answer your question no matter what because, they want the job which you are offering. But, once you hire them then they are gonna revert their true nature. So, if you want to hire people who posses teamwork traits, You need to change your interview process and interview question. For example, Ask someone to tell you about someone who is better than they are in a area that they care about. People who are humble will answer this because they can recognize the strings and others without feeling threatened. To look for hunger you might ask for the hardest they have ever worked for something. Look for evidence of sacrifice and drive. From their answer you can get to know insights of his answer. Remember,

“You need people with you who could have moved heaven and Earth to make your dream come true.”

If you ask them to the point that, what sort of people annoys you and how do they deal with those people? will give you an idea about their emotional intelligence that is their people smartness. There are many such questions you can make to make sure your hire is always best fit to your team and vision. And anyone is not fitting in this matrix of “Team Player” deserves to get fired from your group as all they can do is damage to your vision and other team mates.
